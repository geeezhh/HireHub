The slow-reply tax

Candidates infer company culture from the first touchpoint. When the median acknowledgement stretches past 48 hours, drop-off spikes — not because people are impatient, but because silence reads as disorganisation.

Try a triage inbox: auto-acknowledge with a human tone, route by role family, and set SLAs visible to the whole team.

Speed is a proxy for respect. You do not need a perfect answer on hour one — you need a human signal.
— HireHub editorial, from 2,400 recruiter interviews

Async interviews that feel fair

Recorded questions can feel cold. The fix is structure: time boxes, retries for technical glitches, and rubrics shared upfront. Candidates perform better when they know how they will be scored.

We recommend capping async stages at 25 minutes and always offering a live alternative for accessibility.

Closing the feedback loop

Rejection without rationale burns trust. A two-sentence, non-automated snippet — even templated — reduces negative reviews and increases re-applications within 12 months.

HireHub nudges employers with gentle reminders when a role closes. Small nudge, outsized goodwill.
